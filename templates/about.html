What is Menstruation?

Menstruation is a natural process that occurs in women's bodies, where the uterus sheds its lining, resulting in bleeding. This process typically occurs every 28 days, but can vary from woman to woman.

Menstruation day

Menstruation Day or Menstrual Awareness Day is observed every year on May 28th. This day is celebrated to raise awareness about menstruation and promote women's health and well-being. During this day, various organizations and communities organize different programs to raise awareness about menstruation. These programs include education about menstruation, access to health services, and protection of women's rights. With a focus on addressing these issues, we are creating this website to solve all the problems faced by women related to menstruation and provide them with a safe and supportive platform.

Phases of Menstruation

    Menstrual Preparation: Hormonal changes prepare the body for menstruation.
    Bleeding: The uterus sheds its lining, resulting in bleeding that exits the vagina.
    Cleaning: The body undergoes a cleaning process, where the uterine lining is shed.
    Healing: The body heals and regenerates the uterine lining.

Symptoms of Menstruation

Abdominal pain
Fatigue
Mood swings
Changes in appetite
Breast tenderness

Health Tips During Menstruation

    Drink plenty of water
    Eat a balanced diet
    Exercise regularly
    Reduce stress
    Get enough sleep
